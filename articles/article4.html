Simple Home Workouts to Stay Fit

By Alex Porter, PT • March 23, 2024 • 4 Min Read

A gym membership is heavily overrated for the average person looking to stay healthy. Your own body weight provides more than enough resistance to build muscle, burn fat, and improve cardiovascular health.

The 20-Minute Full Body Routine

If you don't have equipment, you can cycle through these four fundamental movements. Perform each exercise for 45 seconds, rest for 15 seconds, and repeat the circuit 4 times.

  • Push-ups: Great for chest, shoulders, and triceps. Modify on knees if needed.
  • Bodyweight Squats: Activates your glutes, quads, and hamstrings.
  • Plank: The ultimate core stabilizer. Ensure your back remains flat.
  • Jumping Jacks: Keeps your heart rate elevated for aerobic benefits.
Pro Tip: Consistency is far more important than intensity. Doing a 15-minute workout 5 days a week yields better results over time than doing a 2-hour workout once every two weeks.

Don't Forget to Stretch

Flexibility is a critical component of fitness. Spend at least 5 minutes post-workout performing static stretches. This helps clear out lactic acid and dramatically reduces the risk of injury over time.
