Why Sleep is Crucial for Your Immune System

By Dr. Emily Vance • March 24, 2024 • 5 Min Read

We often treat sleep as a luxury rather than a necessity. However, science reveals that sleep is the ultimate biological reset button, deeply interconnected with our immune system.

Cellular Repair in Deep Sleep

During the deepest stages of sleep, your body goes to work. Heart rate drops, muscles relax, and your immune system actively releases proteins called cytokines, some of which help promote sleep and combat infection or inflammation.

Medical Fact: Sleep deprivation directly decreases the production of protective cytokines and infection-fighting antibodies.

How Much Sleep Do We Need?

The optimal amount varies, but most healthy adults need between 7 and 9 hours of sleep per night to function at their best. Children and teenagers need even more.

3 Tips for Better Sleep Hygiene

  • Set a schedule: Go to bed and wake up at the exact same time every day.
  • Avoid Screens: Blue light from phones mimics daylight, disrupting melatonin production.
  • Cool Environment: Keep your bedroom completely dark and relatively cool (around 65°F / 18°C).
